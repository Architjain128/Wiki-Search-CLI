the '''zahliote group – zg''' ([[arabic]]: مجموعة زحلوتي | ''majmueat zhlouty''), known also as the '''groupement zahliote (gz)''' in [[french language|french]], was a small [[christianity in lebanon|lebanese christian]] militia raised in the [[melkite christianity in lebanon|greek-catholic]] town of [[zahlé]] in the [[beqaa valley]], which fought in the [[lebanese civil war]] from 1975 to 1981.

==structure and organization==
the zg was led by aziz wardah, a wealthy banker and entrepreneur, who formed it in 1975 as a movement of middle-class businessmen who contested the rule of the local feudal clans, gathered in the so-called 'seven families' coalition headed by the [[za'im system|za'im]] (political boss) [[joseph skaff]]. wardah's zahliotes were estimated at about 100-500 fighters equipped with small-arms purchased on the black market or taken from [[lebanese army]] depots and [[internal security forces]] (isf) police stations, backed by a few [[gun-truck]]s or [[technical (vehicle)|technicals]] ([[willys m38a1|willys m38a1 md jeep]]s, [[toyota land cruiser (j40)]] and [[land rover series|land-rover series ii-iii pickup]]s, and [[dodge d series|dodge d series (3rd generation) light pickups]]) fitted with [[heavy machine-guns]] and [[recoilless rifles]], controlled most of [[zahlé]] until 1978, when they were finally absorbed into the [[lebanese forces (militia)|lebanese forces]].

==the zahliotes in the lebanese civil war (1975-1981)==
on 28 august 1975, the zahliote group militia clashed at [[zahlé]] with the predominantly [[maronite christianity in lebanon|maronite]] [[zgharta liberation army]] (zla, a.k.a. the "marada brigade") militia led by [[tony frangieh]], despite the intervention of [[lebanese army]] troops in a vain attempt to curb the fighting.<ref>hokayem, ''l'armée libanaise pendant la guerre: un instrument du pouvoir du président de la république (1975-1985)'' (2012), p. 20.</ref> allied with the other rightist [[christianity in lebanon|christian]] factions in the [[lebanese front]], the zahliotes held their ground successfully against the plo, the leftist [[islam in lebanon|muslim]] [[lebanese national movement]] (lnm) militias and [[lebanese arab army]] (laa) attempts to take zahlé in early 1976. on 14 january that year, they defended their town when it was besieged by plo – lnm forces in retaliation for the fall of the [[palestinian refugee camps|palestinian refugee camp]] of [[dbayeh]] in the hands of the [[lebanese front]]'s christian militias earlier that same day.<ref>hokayem, ''l'armée libanaise pendant la guerre: un instrument du pouvoir du président de la république (1975-1985)'' (2012), p. 21.</ref> although the zg was integrated into the [[lebanese forces (militia)|lebanese forces]] structure in 1978, its former members certainly played a role in the defence of their town on 20 december 1980, when the [[tigers militia|free tigers militia]] (a.k.a. the "hannache group") managed to seize by force the local [[national liberal party (lebanon)|national liberal party]] (nlp) offices<ref>mclaurin, ''the battle of zahle'' (1986), pp. 6-7.</ref><ref>menargues, ''les secrets de la guerre du liban'' (2004), p. 57.</ref> and again in march 1981, when it was besieged by the [[syrian army]] during the [[battle of zahleh]].<ref>katz, russel, and volstad, ''armies in lebanon 1982-84'' (1985), p. 8.</ref>
